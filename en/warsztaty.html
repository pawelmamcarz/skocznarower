Regular dirt, slopestyle and pumptrack riding lessons for kids and teens. Sessions twice a week, 90 minutes each, in small groups of up to 6 riders, at three skill levels: from first laps of the pumptrack to confident airtime on the jumps. Grodzisk Mazowiecki and Milanówek. The first test groups start in August 2026, sign-ups for the 50 zł trial session are already open.

What the workshops are

A steady, regular training program. Your child joins a fixed small group matched to age and skill, has a regular coach and a clear progression path: every skill is first practised on flat ground or on a small feature, and only then taken to the track. Progress shows week to week, and each completed level ends with moving up to the next group.

In short: 2 sessions a week, 90 minutes each, up to 6 riders per group, a program split into three levels. There is one entry requirement: the child rides a bike confidently on their own.

The program grew out of the skocznarower.pl bike service. Mateusz (xPyszczek) has been riding dirt and BMX himself for years, so course riders have workshop backup on site: a check-up before the start, a quick repair when something breaks during training, and a discount on parts and accessories for course riders.

Three levels

Start

Basics · pumptrack

For kids who ride confidently but have never trained technique.

  • basic and active position
  • pumping the rollers
  • balance and body work in the berms
  • bunny hop on flat ground
  • first full laps of the pumptrack

Progress

Intermediate · skatepark and small jumps

For riders who flow around the pumptrack and are ready for their first jumps.

  • manual and holding balance
  • small jumps and landings
  • drops: technique and confidence
  • flow at the skatepark
  • preparing for dirt lines

Air

Advanced · dirt and slopestyle

For teens 13+ who already jump confidently and want to seriously develop tricks.

  • airtime: bike control in the air
  • tabletop, whip
  • full dirt lines
  • trips to a bike park
  • preparing for first competitions

Group placement happens at the trial session, based on skill, not just age. Moving up a level follows once the progression is complete.

Safety and gear

Dirt and slopestyle taught with shortcuts end in injury. That is why the program is built around progression: no element gets skipped, a new skill comes in only when the previous one is solid. On top of that:

  • Helmet required at every session, no exceptions (EN 1078 standard). For riders under 16, a bike helmet is also a legal requirement in Poland from 3 June 2026.
  • On the jumps and at the skatepark: full-face helmet and knee pads, elbow pads recommended. On site we only rent helmets; knee and elbow pads must be your own.
  • Small groups: up to 6 riders per coach, fewer at the Air level.
  • A working bike: we check its condition before the first session. Brakes and a tight cockpit are the condition for getting on the track.
  • Paperwork: written parental consent, a declaration of no medical contraindications and accident (NNW) insurance for participants. Consent to publish photos and video is separate and voluntary.
  • Coach trained in first aid: first-aid kit and an accident procedure at every session.

FAQ

From what age can I sign my child up?

From 7 to 17 years old. We assign groups primarily by skill, not just by age. There is one entry requirement: the child must ride a bike confidently on their own (starting, braking, turning without help).

Does my child need their own bike?

Ideally, bring their own working bike: an MTB hardtail or a dirt bike. On the pumptrack almost any working bike will do. For rent we only have BMX bikes at 65 zł; at the Start level they are enough, higher up an own MTB or dirt bike works better. We check the bike's condition before the first session; the semester pass includes a free check-up at the service.

What protective gear is required?

A helmet at every session, mandatory (EN 1078 standard; for riders under 16 it is a legal requirement in Poland from 3 June 2026). On the jumps and at the skatepark a full-face helmet and knee pads, elbow pads recommended. On site we only rent helmets; knee and elbow pads must be your own.

Where do the sessions take place?

At venues in Grodzisk Mazowiecki (pumptrack) and in Milanówek (skatepark and pumptrack at Turczynek). The advanced group additionally travels for sessions at a bike park. You get the exact group schedule at sign-up.

How much does it cost?

The first trial session (60 minutes) is 50 zł. A monthly pass (8 sessions) is 560 zł, which works out at 70 zł per session. A single session is 90 zł, individual training (90 minutes) 200 zł, day camps from 1090 zł for 5 days. Course riders get a 10% discount on service and accessories.

How do I sign up?

The easiest way is the sign-up form on this page: you give your child's age, level and preferred location, and we call back with a group proposal. You can also message us on WhatsApp or call: 600 370 810. Note: the bike service booking form (/umow) is not for workshop sign-ups.

What about winter?

The program runs year-round. From November to February we move part of the sessions to indoor skateparks in Warsaw and replace part with general fitness training for riding. During winter break we run day camps.

Will my child learn to jump from zero?

Yes, that is what the program is for. It starts with position and pumping on the pumptrack, then bunny hop, manual and small jumps, and only at the end full-size jumps. The pace depends on the child; typically the first confident jumps come after 3-5 months of regular training.
