What brake bleeding is

Bleeding means purging air from a hydraulic brake: replacing or topping up the oil in the brake line so no air bubbles remain in the system. Air compresses, oil does not. As long as the brake holds nothing but oil, the lever feels firm and precise. Once air collects in the line, the lever pulls back to the handlebar and braking becomes spongy and unpredictable.

In short: bleeding is a service procedure hydraulic brakes need roughly once every year or two, depending on how hard you ride. Without a bleed the brake gets steadily worse, until in the worst case it stops braking on a descent.

Bleeding Shimano brakes works differently than SRAM, because Shimano uses mineral oil and SRAM uses DOT fluid. Two completely different fluids that must never be mixed. Shimano brakes are bled bottom-up: oil goes in through the caliper and air escapes at the lever through a dedicated funnel.

When a bleed is needed

The simplest signs that a brake needs bleeding:

  • The lever pulls to the bar. You squeeze, the lever travels close to the grip, yet braking stays weak. The classic symptom of air in the system.
  • Spongy feel. The brake is not as firm as it should be. The lever works soft and can be squeezed further than it used to.
  • The bite point wanders when you pump the lever. After a few pulls the point where the lever stops shifts. A sign there is air in the system rearranging itself.
  • The brake gets worse on descents. On the flat it seems fine, but on a long descent (it heats up) it starts to give way. The technical term: brake fade.
  • As prevention, once every year or two. Even when the brake seems to work, the oil absorbs moisture over time. Old oil has a lower boiling point, so it forms vapour bubbles sooner during prolonged braking.

If you can see leaks at the lever or the caliper, a bleed alone will not do: the seals need replacing. During service I always check the state of the pistons and seals and tell you what I found before doing any further work.

Why have your brakes bled by a specialist

In theory you can bleed Shimano brakes at home with a shop kit (funnel + syringe). In practice it usually ends with:

  • Air staying in the caliper, the brake still spongy.
  • Oil spilling onto the pads, the pads going in the bin.
  • The lever not sitting at the right height relative to the caliper, so part of the air stays trapped in the line.
  • No certainty whether the new oil really replaced the old one or just diluted it.

What you get here:

  • The full Shimano tool set: funnel, syringes, tools for the caliper bleed blocks.
  • Genuine Shimano mineral oil (no substitutes).
  • A work stand that sets the lever and caliper at the correct angle.
  • The manufacturer's procedure followed step by step.
  • A post-bleed test: several hard pulls and a check that the bite point does not move.

The result: a brake as firm as new, a lever that works where it should, and the same braking on the first descent and the fiftieth.

FAQ

How can you tell a brake needs bleeding?

The most common symptoms: the lever pulls to the bar, a spongy feel, the point where the lever stops wanders when you pump it, brake fade on a long descent. As prevention it is worth bleeding once every year or two, because mineral oil absorbs moisture over time and its boiling point drops.

Bleeding one circuit from 100 zł, bleeding both circuits from 180 zł, a full oil change (drain + bleed) from 130 zł per circuit, a bleed with brake hose replacement from 160 zł per circuit. You get an exact quote after inspection, before any work is done.

How long does brake bleeding take?

Bleeding one circuit on a healthy system takes around 30-45 minutes. Both circuits: an hour, an hour and a half. If the system needs extra work (leaks, hose replacement, piston cleaning), a little longer. In most cases I do the bleed the same day you drop the bike off.

Does bleeding increase braking power?

Bleeding restores the full power and precision the brake had when it was new. If the pads are worn or the rotors are warped, a bleed alone will not fix that, those parts need replacing as well.

Which Shimano oil do you use?

Genuine Shimano mineral oil. Some alternatives work technically, but for servicing I stick to what the manufacturer approves, because that guarantees temperature stability.

Do you bleed SRAM, TRP, Hope, Magura brakes?

Yes, I also bleed SRAM (DOT 5.1 fluid), TRP, Hope and Magura. Each manufacturer has its own procedure and I use the correct fluid and tools for each of them. Pricing is analogous: from 100 zł per circuit.
